# Code of conduct

## Our commitment

We aim for a respectful, inclusive, technically rigorous community where people can learn and contribute
without harassment, intimidation, or exploitation. Speech technology affects speakers and listeners who
may never participate in the repository; respect for their consent, privacy, identity, and safety is part
of community conduct.

## Expected behavior

- Communicate constructively and critique ideas/code rather than people.
- Welcome questions and explain assumptions without gatekeeping.
- Credit work and disclose relevant conflicts or limitations.
- Protect private information, credentials, recordings, transcripts, and vulnerability details.
- Use synthetic or properly authorized examples.
- Treat consent and responsible-use concerns as engineering requirements, not obstacles to dismiss.
- Accept moderator decisions and repair harm when mistakes occur.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, doxxing, stalking, personal attacks, sustained
disruption, publishing private data, bad-faith manipulation, and retaliation are unacceptable. So are
requests/contributions that facilitate non-consensual voice cloning, deceptive impersonation, fraud,
biometric bypass, harassment, or evasion of safeguards.

## Scope and enforcement

This policy applies in repository discussions, code review, chat/events, issue reports, and public
representation of the project. Report conduct concerns privately to maintainers. Security vulnerabilities
should follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md); urgent voice misuse/takedown reports should be handled privately
and promptly.

Maintainers should protect reporter confidentiality, assess context and impact, avoid conflicts of
interest, document decisions, and take proportionate action: clarification, warning, content removal,
temporary restriction, permanent ban, or escalation to platform/organizational safety channels. They may
reject or remove content that threatens people, rights, project integrity, or responsible use even when
it is technically sophisticated.

Good-faith disagreement about model/design choices is welcome. This policy is not a tool for suppressing
evidence-based criticism or vulnerability reporting.
